Mission:

Our mission is to connect people to powerful, impassioned and insightful stories. We believe in storytelling's capacity to layer narratives. To call to action. To uplift. To break barriers.

Why?

Annually, the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (UNOCHA) releases the Global Humanitarian Overview, which details enduring humanitarian crises. We saw it. We wanted to learn more. So, we built a tool. And now we want to share that tool with you.

What?

Our algorithm analyzes winners of the Pulitzer Prize for International Reporting and captures a similarity score for any article that we feed it. Curently, we use it to suggest powerful articles about pressing humanitarian crises.

Frequently Asked Questions

What next?
There is a lot of work to do. We are currently working on improving the algorithm's capacity to make powerful suggestions and ignore extraneous, unrelated articles, for example. As far as we are concerned, however, the algorithim is just a start. There are a lot of unanswered questions begging to be broached and a couple hundred lines of code can only do so much to answer them. We cannot wait to see where our ideas take us.
How does the algorithim work? What metrics do you use?
Our algorithim uses a powerful neural network library written in Python: Keras. We compute lingual and sentiment data (we use TextBlob for the latter, a natural language processing toolkit) for Pulitzer-winning and non-Pulitzer-winning articles alike. We train a model, which, combined with our ranking algorithim, produces a set of ranked articles. These articles are then stored in our database.

It is not perfect and we are always looking for ways to improve it, but it does generate powerful suggestions and we are beyond excited to share it with you in this capacity. This, however, is a fairly surface-level explanation of the technology, so if you would like to learn more, please reach out to john@articulyze.com or na@articulyze.com.

Why?

Annually, the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (UNOCHA) releases the Global Humanitarian Overview. We saw it. We wanted to learn more. So, we built a tool. And now we want to share that tool with you.

What?

Our algorithm analyzes winners of the Pulitzer Prize for International Reporting and captures a similarity score for any article that we feed it. Curently, we use it to suggest powerful articles about pressing humanitarian crises.

How does the algorithim work? What metrics do you use?
Our algorithim uses a powerful neural network library written in Python, Keras. We compute lingual and sentiment data (we use TextBlob for the latter, a natural language processing toolkit) for a lot of articles, Pulitzer-winning and otherwise. We train a model, which, combined with our ranking algorithim, produces ranked articles. These articles are then stored in our database.

It is not perfect and we are always looking for ways to improve it, but it does generate powerful suggestions and we are beyond excited to share it with you in this capacity.
